JLPT Overview

The JLPT (Japanese-Language Proficiency Test) is the worldwide standard for measuring Japanese ability.

The JLPT has five levels. N5 is the easiest, and N1 is the most difficult.

Level Goal & Proficiency Kanji (Approx.) Vocabulary (Approx.)
N5 Beginner: Basic sentences, Hiragana, Katakana, and basic Kanji. 100 800
N4 Elementary: Basic daily topics and slightly more complex grammar. 300 1,500
N3 Intermediate: Bridge between basic and business Japanese. Understands daily conversation. 600 3,000
N2 Pre-Advanced: Business level. Can read newspapers and understand complex topics. 1,000 6,000
N1 Advanced: Native-like fluency. Can understand abstract and professional topics. 2,000 10,000

Rules & Regulations

The Japanese-Language Proficiency Test (JLPT) is held worldwide to evaluate Japanese skills. Ensure you have your voucher and valid ID. No electronic devices are permitted during the sections.

Mark Evaluation

Scoring is based on Scaled Scores, not raw marks. You must pass every individual section (Language Knowledge, Reading, Listening) to pass the overall exam.

Life in Japan

Japan balances ancient tradition with cutting-edge technology. From the quiet temples of Kyoto to the neon lights of Shibuya, the culture emphasizes respect, punctuality, and seasonal beauty.

Famous Food: Sushi, Ramen, Takoyaki, and Wagyu Beef.
